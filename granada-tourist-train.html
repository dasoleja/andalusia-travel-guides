Granada Tourist Train (Ruta Verde - Green Route)

A Scenic 1.5-Hour Loop Through Granada's Historic Neighborhoods

At a Glance

Essential Details

  • Duration: 1 hour 25 minutes (continuous loop, no stops required)
  • Fare: €8-10 per person (children under 5 often free)
  • Included in Granada Card: Yes—tourist train is part of your card
  • Route Type: Continuous circular route with 10 stops
  • Departs From: Near Plaza Nueva (city center, walking distance from Cathedral)
  • Best Time to Go: Early morning (8:00-9:00 AM) or late afternoon (4:30-5:30 PM) to avoid peak midday heat
Perfect for Your Group: The train is ideal for families with young children. Your 3-year-old can relax while seeing the city. The continuous 90-minute ride is manageable, and commentary is usually in Spanish + English.
The 10 Stops (Ruta Verde - Green Route)

The Route Explained

The train makes a circular loop, hitting these 10 neighborhood stops. It starts near Plaza Nueva and returns to the same area. You don't need to get off at stops—the train is continuous—but knowing the route helps you orient to Granada.

01 Alhambra / Generalife — The palace complex entrance at the edge of the city
02 Alhambra / Puerta de la Justicia — Alternative Alhambra entrance (Justice Gate)
03 Plaza Nueva — Downtown Granada's main square and cultural heart
04 Alhóndiga — Historic market area with traditional Granada architecture
05 Cathedral / Capilla Real — Granada Cathedral and Royal Chapel where the Catholic Monarchs are buried
06 Mirador de San Cristóbal / Jardines de Zoraya — Scenic viewpoint overlooking the city and Alhambra from the north
07 Arco de Elvira / Hospital Real — Historic gate and royal hospital (now a cultural center)
08 Calle Reyes Católicos / Plaza Isabel la Católica — Historic royal avenue with monuments to the Catholic Monarchs
09 Calle Molinos / Campo del Príncipe — Local neighborhood with traditional charm
10 Carmen de los Mártires — Historic palace and gardens (beautiful photo spot)
What You'll Experience

A Leisurely Orientation to Granada

Unlike walking tours, the tourist train lets you sit, relax, and see multiple neighborhoods in 90 minutes. You'll pass through the Albaicín (narrow white-washed streets), see the Alhambra from multiple angles, cross the Guadalquivir River, and loop through both historic and modern Granada.

Commentary & Audio

The train has recorded commentary in Spanish and English explaining each stop and the history of Granada. It's informative without being overwhelming. Younger kids will probably tune out the historical details but will enjoy the ride itself.

Best Vantage Points

  • Alhambra Views: You'll see the palace from multiple angles throughout the loop
  • Albaicín Neighborhood: The old medina with white cubic houses cascading down the hillside—very picturesque
  • Sacromonte: The cave monastery district on the hillside opposite the Alhambra
  • Mirador de San Cristóbal (Stop 6): The best sunset photo spot if you ride in late afternoon
Practical Information

Boarding & Payment

  • Granada Card: Simply show your card at the station; entry is included
  • Where to Board: Main station is near Plaza Nueva (Calle Reyes Católicos or nearby)
  • Frequency: Trains usually run every 20-30 minutes
  • Wait Time: 10-20 minutes typically

Strollers & Accessibility

  • Strollers: Fits on the train; usually space for 1-2 per car
  • Wheelchairs: Limited accessibility; ask staff when boarding
  • Seating: Open-air train with bench seating; minimal shade (bring sunscreen and hats)

Comfort & Heat

  • No Climate Control: It's an open-air train—you'll feel the heat
  • Sun Exposure: Bring hats, sunscreen, water for the 90-minute ride
  • Noise: The train is somewhat loud; conversation will be difficult during the ride

Photography

Excellent photo opportunities, especially at stops. Best shots: Alhambra from different angles, Albaicín neighborhoods, city panoramas.

Heat & Comfort Strategy (July 30 or Aug 1-3)
Heads Up: An open-air train in 38-40°C (100-104°F) heat is challenging. Plan strategically.

Timing Recommendations

  • Early Morning (8:00-9:30 AM): Best option. Cooler, fewer crowds, you see the city fresh.
  • Late Afternoon (4:30-6:00 PM): Second choice. Sun is lower, shadows cool things slightly, golden light for photos.
  • Avoid Midday (12:00-4:00 PM): Peak heat + sun directly overhead = miserable for kids
For Your 3-Year-Old: The ride might be challenging in peak heat. Consider going early morning if possible. Bring water, a portable fan if you have one, and plan a lunch/indoor break right after.

What to Bring

  • High-SPF sunscreen (reapply midway through)
  • Hats for everyone
  • 2+ liters of water
  • Light snacks
  • Lightweight, loose-fitting clothing
How It Fits Your Trip

Optional, Not Required

The tourist train is included in your Granada Card, so if you want to do it, it's already paid for. It's a nice way to:

  • Get oriented to Granada's layout before exploring on foot
  • See neighborhoods (Albaicín, Sacromonte) without walking uphill in heat
  • Give your 3-year-old a break from walking
  • Take scenic photos from a moving vantage point

Skip It If...

  • Your group is exhausted and needs rest instead
  • Heat concerns make open-air sitting unpleasant
  • You prefer walking to explore at your own pace
Suggestion: Consider the tourist train if you have a rest day or afternoon free. It's low-energy and good for kids, but skip it if everyone is already heat-exhausted.
