Seville Cathedral

One of Europe's Largest & Home to Columbus's Remains

At a Glance

Essential Details

  • Location: Avenida de la Constitución, Seville 41004
  • Built: 1402-1517 CE (on site of Almohad Mosque)
  • Size: One of Europe's largest cathedrals by area
  • Entry: €5 (same ticket as Giralda climb)
  • Hours: 11:00 AM - 5:00 PM (summer)
  • Estimated Time: 1-1.5 hours (more if you read all inscriptions)
  • Best Time: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and heat
UNESCO World Heritage Site: The Cathedral and Giralda are jointly listed as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. This is Spain's most visited cathedral.
What You'll See

The Vastness

When you enter, the sheer scale hits you. The cathedral was designed to overwhelm—to express Christian power and permanence after the conquest of Granada. High vaulted ceilings, soaring columns, golden altars visible from far away. It's the opposite of Islamic simplicity.

Columbus's Tomb (The Catafalco)

One of the most important objects in the cathedral. A dramatic monument supporting a casket supposedly containing Columbus's remains. Four sculptures represent the crowns of the kingdoms that funded his voyage. It's ornate and imposing—a monument to the age of exploration that began when Granada fell in 1492.

Fun Fact: Scholars debate whether Columbus is actually here or in the Dominican Republic. But Seville claims him, and the monument is impressive regardless.

The Altarpiece (Retablo Mayor)

One of Spain's largest and most ornate altarpieces. Gilded wood carvings, hundreds of biblical scenes, intricate details. It took decades to complete. If you squint, you can make out individual carved figures in the golden mass.

Stained Glass & Light

Large stained glass windows filter the intense Seville sunlight into colored beams. The interplay of light creates a contemplative atmosphere—a sharp contrast to the heat outside.

Historical Context

Built on a Mosque

The cathedral was constructed on the site of the Almohad Mosque. Unlike the Córdoba Mezquita, where the mosque was preserved, the Seville mosque was demolished. However, the Giralda (the minaret) was kept—too magnificent to destroy, and by then the Spanish were confident in their dominance.

Timing: 1492 & After

Construction began in 1402 (before Granada fell), but accelerated after 1492. The conquest of Granada in January 1492 energized Christian Spain. The same year, Columbus sailed westward, expanding Spanish power globally. Seville, as the embarkation port, became incredibly wealthy. This wealth funded the cathedral's completion and decoration.

Seville as Colonial Capital

From 1500-1650, Seville was the wealthiest city in Spain, the center of colonial trade. All Spanish colonial goods flowed through Seville's harbor. The cathedral reflects this wealth—it's not just impressive, it's ostentatious. Gold, gems, ornate carvings—it's a statement of power.

Connection to Your Trip: Visit this after the Córdoba Mezquita and Granada Cathedral to see how Christian Spain used architecture as conquest. The contrast between Islamic restraint (Córdoba, Alhambra) and Christian baroque excess (Seville, Granada Cathedral) is striking.
Practical Information

Stroller & Accessibility

  • Strollers: Flat, wide floors; stroller-friendly throughout
  • Crowds: Can get busy; visit early to avoid
  • Silence: Expected due to sacred nature; keep children calm
Tip: This is one of the most stroller-friendly cathedrals on your trip. Wide aisles, flat terrain, no tight passages.

Dress Code

  • Required: Shoulders and knees covered
  • Respectful Behavior: Quiet voices, slow movement

What's Special

  • Free Exploration: No guided tour required; you can roam freely
  • Photography: Generally allowed in most areas (flash may be discouraged)
  • Time: Spend as much or as little as you want; can be done in 45 min or 2 hours
Heat Management (July 28-31)
Best Aspect: The cathedral interior is naturally cool (thick stone walls) and a perfect heat refuge. You'll feel 10°C cooler inside than on the street.

Strategy

  • Visit during the hottest part of your day (2:00-4:00 PM) for maximum cooling benefit
  • Take your time; walk slowly and let the cool air soak in
  • Sit quietly for 10 minutes if the kids are getting tired—it's peaceful and cool
